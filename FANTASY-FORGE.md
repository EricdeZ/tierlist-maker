# The Fantasy Forge — Whitepaper

## Simple Explanation

The Fantasy Forge is a player loyalty game built into the SMITE 2 Companion. You spend **Passion** to **Fuel** players you believe in, receiving **Sparks** in return. Each player has a **Heat** — a number representing how valued they are in the Forge. Heat rises when more people Fuel a player, and shifts up or down based on how well they perform in matches compared to others in their role.

If a player's Heat rises after you Fuel them, your Sparks become worth more Passion than you originally spent. You can **Cool** your Sparks to reclaim Passion — ideally more than you put in. If Heat drops, you'll get back less.

| Action | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Fuel** | Commit Passion to a player, receive Sparks |
| **Cool** | Release your Sparks, reclaim Passion |
| **Sparks** | Your connection to a player |
| **Heat** | A player's current worth in the Forge |
| **My Sparks** | Your active Forge positions |
| **Cooling Tax** | A 10% fee when Cooling |

The Forge runs per-season. When a season ends, all Sparks are automatically released — everyone receives their Passion back at current Heat, tax-free.

**Most importantly:** There is no real money involved in the Fantasy Forge whatsoever. There are no microtransactions, no purchases, no premium currency — nothing. Passion is earned entirely through engagement with the SMITE 2 Companion (daily logins, saving tier lists, completing challenges, etc.). The Forge is purely a loyalty and engagement feature.

---

## Goal

The Fantasy Forge gives users a reason to **watch the games**. When you have Sparks on a player, you care how they perform — not just whether their team wins, but how well they individually play. Users will tune into league Twitch streams to scout players, track performance trends, and look for undervalued talent before others catch on.

If the Forge gains traction, this translates directly into **higher viewership and deeper engagement** for league streams. Every match becomes meaningful to a wider audience — not just fans of the two teams playing, but anyone with Sparks riding on a player in that game.

## How Heat Works

Every player enters the Forge at a base Heat of **100**. Two forces move it from there:

### 1. Demand

When someone Fuels a player, new Sparks are created. When someone Cools, Sparks are destroyed. The more Sparks a player has, the higher their Heat. Each outstanding Spark adds **1%** to a player's Heat.

A player with 50 Sparks has 50% more Heat than base. A player with 200 Sparks has 200% more. This means each successive Spark costs more than the last — the Forge naturally resists anyone from dominating a single player.

There is no cap on how many Sparks a player can have. The rising cost handles that on its own.

### 2. Performance

After each match, the Forge evaluates how every player performed compared to others in the **same role** during that **same week**. A player who outperforms their role peers sees their Heat rise. One who underperforms sees it cool off.

The system looks at a player's overall match contribution — combat effectiveness, damage output, and damage absorbed — and compares it against the weekly average for their role. An average performance scores a 1.0. Above-average scores higher; below-average scores lower.

---

## The Self-Balancing Forge

The performance adjustment is **relative to a player's current Heat**. High-Heat players are expected to perform proportionally better to maintain their position.

A player at Heat 200 is expected to perform twice as well as an average player. If they perform just average, their Heat drifts downward. A player at Heat 50 only needs to perform half as well as average to hold steady — and average performance would push them upward.

**What this means in practice:**

| Situation | What happens |
|---|---|
| Popular player, average week | Heat drifts down — they're expected to do better |
| Fairly heated player, average week | Heat stays the same |
| Underappreciated player, strong week | Heat rises quickly |
| Expensive player, dominant week | Heat holds steady — great stats match expectations |
| Cold player, weak week | Heat drops further |

This keeps the Forge self-correcting. Hype alone can't sustain a player's Heat forever — they need the stats to back it up. And overlooked players who perform well will naturally rise.

Performance shifts are dampened so no single week causes a dramatic swing. Heat adjustments are gradual, rewarding sustained performance over time.

## How Fueling Works

When you Fuel a player, you choose how many Sparks to commit (up to 10 at a time). Each Spark costs slightly more than the last because the player's Heat increases with each one.

**Example:** A player has 30 Sparks at Heat 130. You Fuel 3 Sparks:
- Spark 1: 130 Passion
- Spark 2: 131 Passion
- Spark 3: 132 Passion
- Total: 393 Passion

The Passion is deducted from your balance immediately.

## How Cooling Works

When you Cool, your Sparks are released. Each successive Spark returns slightly less because the player's Heat decreases as Sparks are removed. A **10% Cooling Tax** is applied to your total.

**Example:** You Cool 3 Sparks from a player with 50 Sparks at Heat 150:
- Spark 1: 149 Passion
- Spark 2: 148 Passion
- Spark 3: 147 Passion
- Gross: 444 Passion
- Cooling Tax (10%): 44 Passion
- You receive: 400 Passion

The Cooling Tax removes Passion from the economy permanently — it doesn't go to anyone. This keeps the overall Passion economy healthy.

### Heat Floor

A player's Heat can never drop below **10**, no matter what.

---

## Season Lifecycle

The Forge is tied to a single season:

1. **Season begins** — The Forge opens. Every active player gets a Spark entry at base Heat of 100.
2. **Season active** — Fuel and Cool freely. Performance updates happen after each reported match.
3. **Season ends** — The Forge **auto-liquidates**:
   - All Sparks are released at their current Heat
   - Passion is returned to every holder
   - **No Cooling Tax** on auto-liquidation
   - The Forge closes permanently for that season

Every season is a clean slate.

---

## Starting Sparks

Every user receives **3 free Starting Sparks** when the Forge opens for a season. These can be assigned to any players — no Passion required. This gives everyone a chance to participate in the Forge even if they haven't earned much Passion yet.

Starting Sparks work exactly like regular Sparks in every other way: they contribute to a player's Heat, gain or lose value based on performance, and are auto-liquidated at season end. The only difference is they cost nothing to Fuel.

When a Starting Spark is Cooled or auto-liquidated, the Passion returned is treated as profit — it goes straight to the user's balance.

### Rank Protection

To prevent users from gaming the rank system through the Forge, the first **330 Passion** earned from the Forge (equivalent to 300 base + 10% Cooling Tax) does **not** count towards lifetime earnings. This means a user's rank cannot be inflated simply by cycling free Sparks through the market. Only genuine Forge profits beyond that threshold contribute to rank progression.
